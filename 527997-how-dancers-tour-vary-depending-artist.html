How much do dancers make on tour? Does it vary depending on the artist?

April 26, 2024
blog

Every dancer dreams of being part of a tour as it offers them a chance to showcase their artistry on diverse stages. However, beyond the applause of their beautiful performances lies the intriguing question, "How much do dancers make on tour?"

Dancing is a form of art that captivates audiences. From energetic hip-hop moves to elegant ballet performances, dancers have a unique talent that requires skill and dedication. However, most people have been curious about the financial aspect of the dancing, particularly when they’re part of a touring production.

How much do top dancers get paid?

According to ZipRecruiter, the average hourly pay for a top dancer is $34.26 hourly, which is approximately $71,263 annually. Most professional dancers' salaries currently range between $13.94 to $35.34. This suggests that there may be many opportunities for advancement and increased pay based on years of experience, location, skill level and the artist.

Professional backup dancers earnings on tour

According to ZipRecruiter, the average hourly earnings for professional backup dancers is $19.50, approximately $40,559 annually. The hourly wages of professional backup dancers are as high as $22.12 and as low as $11.54. But depending on the location, artist and type of event, professional backup dancers usually earn more than the stated hourly rate.
