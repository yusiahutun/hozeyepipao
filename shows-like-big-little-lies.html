Addictive Shows like Big Little Lies to Watch Now

January 26, 2024
blog
1

Enlightened

Home Box Office/Kobal/Shutterstock

If you're enamored with Laura Dern's show-stealing performance in Big Little Lies, you're going to love Enlightened. The series ran on HBO for two short seasons, and Dern won a Golden Globe for her performance as impassioned (and very slightly unhinged) office worker Amy Jellicoe. Enlightened was created by Mike White, who's responsible for the epic Jennifer Aniston and Jake Gyllenhaal movie, The Good Girl. Before its time, it's the perfect precursor to Big Little Lies.

WATCH NOW

2

Insecure

Merie W. Wallace/HBO

Partly based upon her web series, The Misadventures of Awkward Black Girl, Issa Rae was catapulted to stardom in her HBO series, Insecure, and the world is better for it. From her complicated love life to her struggle to find a job she actually likes, Rae is endlessly watchable and painfully relatable. If you live for the cringe-worthy and tense interactions on Big Little Lies, then you'll love Insecure.

WATCH NOW

3

The Affair

Showtime

Alison (Ruth Wilson) and Noah (Dominic West) cross paths in Montauk and quickly embark on an affair. Despite being married to Cole, played by heartthrob Joshua Jackson, Alison is grieving the death of her son. BLL fans will particularly enjoy the first two seasons of The Affair, in which detectives try to solve a murder. Season 3 gets clumsy, and 4 is heartbreakingly bad. Season 5 is due in 2019 and costars Anna Paquin.

WATCH NOW

4

How to Get Away with Murder

Disney//ABC

Annalise Keating, played by the inimitable Viola Davis, is a law professor who teaches her students how to get away with murder. Outside of class, her life is pretty much a disaster, and there's more than one murder mystery to solve each season. Executive-produced by Shonda Rhimes, the glossy ABC vehicle finally gives Davis the mainstream platform she deserves. But the upcoming Season 6 will be its last.

WATCH NOW

5

Gypsy

Netflix

Sadly, there's only one season of Netflix's Gypsy, which costars Naomi Watts and Billy Crudup, even though it had all the makings of a twisty thriller. Created by Lisa Rubin, with two episodes directed by BAFTA nominee Sam Taylor-Johnson, Gypsy follows psychologist Jean as she gets inappropriately involved with her patients and their partners. Sophie Cookson plays Sidney, the object of Jean's affection, in an enigmatic and career-making performance.

WATCH NOW

6

Succession

Brian Cox (the original Hannibal Lecter) stars as the patriarch in this show about a seriously rich and morally inept family of media moguls. Kendall Roy (Jeremy Strong) is poised to take over the family business when disaster strikes. While Kieran Culkin provides the wise-cracks, Matthew Macfadyen reminds us that Brits do it better. Season 2 of Succession hits HBO in August 2019.

WATCH NOW

7

Killing Eve

BBC

Created by Phoebe Waller-Bridge of Fleabag fame, BBC America's thriller costars Sandra Oh as MI5 agent Eve Polastri as she tracks down assassin Villanelle, played by Jodie Comer. Killing Eve Season 3 is definitely happening, which will come as a relief to anyone obsessed with the insane chemistry between Comer and Oh. Unsurprisingly, both actresses are up for Emmys in 2019.

WATCH NOW

8

Better Call Saul

Michele K Short/Amc/Kobal/Shutterstock

The prequel to Breaking Bad works as both a spin-off and a standalone drama. Before he became Saul Goodman (Bob Odenkirk), Jimmy McGill was a hapless lawyer intent on impressing his way more successful brother. Kim Wexler (Rhea Seehorn), a fellow associate and sometime love interest, tries to support Jimmy, but his proclivities are a little too much to handle. Season 5 is due in 2020.

WATCH NOW

9

Good Girls

NBC

Three mothers (Christina Hendricks, Mae Whitman, and Retta), who are all short on cash, join forces to pull off a heist in a supermarket. However, their problems begin to spiral when they catch the store manager's attention for the wrong reasons. If you can't get enough of the Monterey Five and the endless familial drama Big Little Lies provides, Good Girls might be just the fix.

WATCH NOW

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

Sharp Objects

HBO

Based on the same-name book by Gillian Flynn (who also penned Gone Girl), Sharp Objects contains an ongoing murder mystery, intense family dynamics, and an intricate protagonist in the form of Amy Adams. Directed by Jean-Marc Vallée, who was responsible for Big Little Lies Season 1, Sharp Objects features sumptuous cinematography, an addictive soundtrack, and the kind of complicated female characters we've dreamed about our entire lives.

WATCH NOW

11

The L Word

Carole Segal/Showtime/Kobal/Shutterstock

Set to return to Showtime in 2019, The L Word is still the only long-running TV show dedicated to telling the stories of gay women. If you watch Big Little Lies for its complex female friendships, then you'll love everything about The L Word. Starring Jennifer Beals of Flashdance fame, the series follows newcomer Jenny as she infiltrates a group of women in Los Angeles and proceeds to write a book about their experiences.

WATCH NOW
