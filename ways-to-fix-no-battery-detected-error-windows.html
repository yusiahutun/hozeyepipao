7 Ways to Fix the No Battery Is Detected Error on Windows

July 22, 2024
blog

One day, you're happily using your Windows device when you suddenly see a scary “No battery is detected” error. Usually, this error message is caused by a defective battery or other system issues.

However, tackling this problem doesn’t always necessarily mean you’ll need to replace the battery.

In this article, we’ll show you the best solutions for resolving the “No battery is detected” error on Windows.

1. Quick Fixes for an Undetected Battery

Before getting into the nitty-gritty of your computer, try the following quick and easy fixes:

Restart Your Laptop

Whenever you encounter system issues like "No battery detected," restarting your Windows device could help. As such, try restarting your PC and check if the issue persists.

Give Your PC Room to Cool

If your device has been exposed to the sun or any form of heat, then the battery might be overheating. In such instances, you might end up seeing the "no battery detected on laptop" or "no batteries are currently installed" error.

So, the best way to fix your overheating PC is to give the device some room to cool.

Clean the Battery Compartment

The battery might be undetected simply because there’s dirt in the battery compartment. So, try cleaning the battery compartment and then check if this resolves the problem.

Reconnect the Battery

You might encounter the "battery not detected" error immediately after accidentally dropping your device. In this instance, you can tackle the issue by simply reconnecting the battery.

2. Check the Battery Status

If you suspect that the battery is damaged, then you could confirm this by checking its status. Also, knowing this will help you understand whether you need to apply troubleshooting fixes or just get a new battery.

So, let’s take a look at how you can check the battery status via the Device Manager:

  • Press Win + R to open the Run command dialog box.
  • Type devmgmt.msc and then press Enter to open the Device Manager.
  • Double-click on the Batteries option to expand it.
  • Right-click on each of the Battery adapters (one at a time) and select Properties from the menu options.

    • In the next window, take a look at the information in the Device status box.

    If the Device status reads, “The device is working properly,” then the battery doesn't have issues. In this case, you can tackle the "No battery detected" error by applying the other solutions in this article.

    If the Device status reads, “The device has a problem,” then the battery is faulty.

    Now, there are two ways to resolve this problem. Firstly, try repairing the battery drivers using the next method. If that doesn't help, then you should consider getting a new battery.

    3. Restart or Reinstall the Battery Drivers

    The “No battery is detected” issue is most likely caused by corrupted battery drivers. So, you could resolve the problem by either restarting or reinstalling the battery drivers.

    Now, let’s take a look at how you can restart the battery drivers:

  • Press Win + X and select Device Manager from the options.
  • Double-click the Batteries option to expand it.
  • Right-click on each of the Battery adapters (one at a time) and select Disable device.
  • Finally, right-click on each Battery adapter again and select Enable device.

    • Restart your PC and check if this resolves the problem.

    If the issue persists, try reinstalling the battery drivers through these steps:

  • Open the Device Manager by applying the previous methods.
  • Right-click on each Battery adapter and select Uninstall device.
  • Click the Scan for hardware changes tab. Wait for the process to complete, and then restart your device.

    • 4. Perform a Power Cycle on Your PC

    When a simple restart doesn’t tackle this problem, you could consider performing a power cycle. During this process, your device will reset and get rid of bugs. So, this could tackle the issue at hand and other system problems.

    Let’s take a look at how you can perform a power cycle on your device:

  • Unplug all the external devices from your PC.
  • Remove the battery and then press the power button for about 10-15 seconds.
  • Re-insert the battery and then reboot your device.
  • Finally, connect your charger and then check if your device can detect the battery.

    • 5. Use the Built-In Windows Troubleshooters

    You can resolve various Windows PC issues by using relevant troubleshooting tools. In this case, we'll explore how the Hardware and Devices and the Power troubleshooters can help resolve the “No battery is detected” error.

    Let's start with the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter:

  • Navigate to Win Start Menu > PC settings > Update & Security and select Troubleshoot on the left-hand side pane.
  • Scroll down on the right-hand side pane and select the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter.
  • Finally, press the Run the troubleshooter button.

    • Next, scroll down and click the Power troubleshooter. From there, press the Run the troubleshooter button.

    Alternatively, you can run the Power troubleshooter via the Control Panel through these steps:

  • Type Control Panel in the Start menu search bar and select the Best match.
  • Click the View by drop-down menu and select Large icons.
  • Scroll down, click the Troubleshooting option, and then click System and Security.
  • Click the Power option.

    • In the pop-up window, click Next and then follow the on-screen instructions to run the Power troubleshooter.

    6. Update the BIOS

    The "No battery is detected" error might sometimes be caused by issues on the device's motherboard. In such cases, updating your PC's BIOS could help tackle this issue. However, ensure that both the battery and the charger are plugged in during this process.

    Updating the BIOS is different for various devices. For example, if your battery is not detected on a Lenovo device, you'll need to go through Lenovo's website to get the latest BIOS update.

    • How to update the BIOS on an ASUS device

    7. Update Windows

    If the battery doesn’t have signs of physical damage, then any of the solutions we’ve covered should help. But if you still can’t resolve the "Windows 10 no battery detected" issue, perhaps your device needs updating.

    Usually, Windows automatically downloads updates for you. However, this depends on how you’ve configured your system settings.

    Now, here are the steps for manually updating your Windows device:

  • Navigate to Win Start Menu > PC Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update.
  • Click the Check for updates button and then follow the on-screen instructions to finalize the process.

    • Your Windows PC Is Finally Detecting Its Battery Again

    The battery is one of the most critical components of your PC. In case it malfunctions, then it becomes a challenge to use your device in areas with no power. Now, you might often come across the “No battery is detected” issue. But there's no need to worry! Simply apply the tips we've covered, and you should be good to go.

    And if your battery seems to have issues, consider checking out the best tools to analyze your PC's battery health.
