The murder suspect of a star university basketball player has been released on a $100,000 bail.

Shanterrica Madden, 18, is accused of killing her roommate, Tina Stewart, after she was found with several stab wounds in the apartment they shared in Murfreesboro, Tennessee.

She had been initially refused bail, but today a judge overturned the original decision.

The funeral of the 21-year-old Middle Tennessee State University Lady Raiders basketball player is set for tomorrow.

The Rutherford County General Sessions Court hearing was postponed until March 24, but the defence and prosecution agreed to a $100,000 bail.

District Attorney General Bill Whitesell said that he understood Miss Stewart's funeral was scheduled for Saturday in Memphis and that the family had scheduled visitation for Friday.

He said he expected Stewart's family would like to be at the preliminary hearing, adding: ‘We certainly want to give them that opportunity.’

Madden’s parents spoke out for the first time since the arrest, claiming their daughter is a family person, a great kid and a good student.

Her mother said: ‘She is focused on life. She knows what she wants out of life.’

Defense attorney Joe Brandon Jr. has said his client acted in self-defence.

Her father, Frank Madden, defended his daughter as someone who has never been a problem and said she planned to become an attorney.

He said the family wants to sit down with their pastor and Miss Stewart's parents to talk soon.

‘That's what we need. We all lost something.’

The alleged incident which happened on March 2, shocked the university.

In a statement posted on the school's website, President Sidney McPhee said: ''The anguish I feel as a father and a president ... but personally as a father with a daughter about the age of Tina cannot adequately be expressed.'

'Our hearts are heavy with grief and our thoughts and prayers are with her and her family.'

Roommates: Tina Stewart, left, was found with stab wounds in the apartment she shared with Shanterrica Madden, right. Madden's first hearing was postponed to allow Stewart's family to attend after the 21-year-old's funeral on Saturday

He described Stewart as a 'wonderfully talented and vibrant young woman.’

Grief counsellors have been put on standby to help students and a candle-lit vigil was held.

Emergency medical teams rushed her to Middle Tennessee Medical Center where she died from her injuries.

Stewart was a junior guard with the Blue Raiders, averaging 5.5 points and 2.5 rebounds in 28 games this season.

Athletic Director Chris Massaro said Stewart's death was an incredible loss, describing her as a 'great kid and a fighter'.

He told the News Journal: 'When I think of her right now, I think of her smile and grin. She would light up a room. It's hard to describe, it's hard for me to find words. I wish you all could have got to know her.

'This is an unspeakable tragedy to one of our family members. The team is devastated, but we'll work through this together ... one step at a time.'
