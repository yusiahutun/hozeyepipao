10 best Black Indie artists and their best songs in 2022

February 17, 2024

The world's best Black indie artists make diverse, exhilarating, compelling, and well-crafted music. These music legends' most guarded secret to producing heart-soothing hits is experimenting with different types of music and genres.

Many upcoming Black indie artists are inspired to grow the genre after observing the many megastars and multimillionaires it has made. The music now has an equally diverse audience, for it has gained acceptance among the most conservative communities worldwide.

Top 10 best Black indie artists in 2022

Black indie artists are getting more famous and wealthier daily. The mushrooming digital platforms like music streaming sites allow independent singers to express their artistry and sell their music without restrictions from recording labels. So, who are the best Black Indie singers at the moment?

She came to the limelight in 2016 when she released a heartfelt contemporary folk-pop song, Waiting Around. The track became a hit when it was used in a TV commercial. Aisha went from writing and literature to playing the guitar and singing while in high school.

She lacked the confidence to apply for music scholarships and decided to join a college in the Bronx. Three years later, Aisha left the institution. She began working at a bookstore while doing open-mic performances (spoken word and music) to raise money for recording her music.

After the 2016 breakthrough, the Nettwerk label signed her up and promoted her Vacancy EP. The singer relocated from New York to Orlando and now enjoys a permaculture life with her partner and children. 2. Arlo Parks

Anaïs Oluwatoyin Estelle Marinho was born on 9th August 2000 (age 21 years as of 2021) in Paris but grew up in Hammersmith, west London. Her parents sent her to a private school called Latymer Upper School.

In 2018, she began uploading demos to BBC Music Introducing. Radio presenters in the UK sent them to Ali Raymond of Beatnik Creative, who later became her manager. In the same year, Arlo Parks released her first single, Cola, and her debut album, Collapsed in Sunbeams, in 2021.

The album got the third position on the UK Albums Chart and received three nominations at the 2021 Brit Awards; Best New Artist, Album of the Year, and Best British Female Solo Artist. She also won the 2021 Hyundai Mercury Prize for best album. The singer centers on indie pop, indie folk, and R&B.

3. Benjamin Booker

Benjamin Booker was born on 4th June 1989 (age 32 years as of 2021) in Virginia Beach, Virginia, USA. He attended DIY punk shows in his teenage years when his family moved to Tampa, Florida.

Booker attended the best school for performing arts, Orange Grove Middle School. He later enrolled at the International Baccalaureate Program in Hillsborough High School and earned a journalism degree from the University of Florida in Gainesville.

Booker still did music shows while working for an NGO. He released his first EP, Waiting Ones, in 2012. The singer combines indie with blues rock, garage rock, and punk blues and keeps his trip mementos at his LA home.

He dropped his funky look to a more basic dressing style in 2017 when he moved from New Orleans to Echo Park to be near his girlfriend. Other Benjamin Booker's songs the world praises are:

4. Melissa Laveaux

Mélissa Michelle Marjolec Laveaux was born on 9th January 1985 (age 37 years in 2022) in Montreal, Canada, to Haitian parents. She toured Haiti in April 2016 at the age of 12 to honor her ancestors and search for her roots.

The trip helped her discover traditional stories and folk songs that nourished Haitian artists for generations. Some of the songs are so old that nobody knows who wrote them. As a result, she reinterprets Haitian heritage (traditional tunes, Vodou anthems, and phrases) from old songbooks and sews them together to make unique tracks.

The singer also got some Creole language vocabularies from the vibrant catchphrases she often heard her mum say over phone calls with her aunt. She is one of the most outstanding Black indie rock artists.

Her first album, Camphor & Copper, came out in 2006, and it was dominated by self-written folk-rock, indie-rock, and pop-rock tracks. Other Melissa Laveaux's songs are:

5. Muhsinah

The Grammy Award-nominated singer and producer was born on 20th July 1983 (age 38 years as of 2021) in Washington DC, USA. She is an alumnus of Duke Ellington School of the Arts and Howard University.

She is among the few talented female singers who are determined to break barriers. Muhsinah found her niche in the competitive music world without being part of any music label. She smoothly mixes electronic beats with R&B and jazz sounds.

Also, Muhsinah's songs have diverse vocals, piano sounds, and synthesizers. XLR8R journal described her 2008 self-produced EP, day.break 2.0, as a beautiful smack in the face of mainstream hip-hop and R&B convention. Other Muhsinah's best-extended plays are:

6. Serpentwithfeet

Josiah Wise, aka Serpentwithfeet, is an experimental pop musician based in Brooklyn in New York City. He was born on 9th July 1988 (age 33 years as of 2021) in Baltimore, Maryland, USA. The singer's dad owned a Christian bookstore while his mum was a choir director.

Josiah sang classical and gospel music growing up, for he was a church choir member. At age 11, he turned down his mum's request to audition for the Maryland State Boychoir. However, she still signed him up, and he later loved the classical vocal music experience it gave him.

The singer left the choir for it lacked diversity and room for a Black voice. The 14-year-old Josiah was part of Baltimore City College's all Black kids choir that won trophies in Europe. He took classical vocal lessons at the University of the Arts in Philadelphia to become a world-class opera performer, but the conservatories he applied to rejected him.

7. Toro Y Moi

Chazwick Bradley Bundick, alias Toro Moi, has a Filipino mum and an African-American dad. He was born on 7th November 1986 (age 35 years as of 2021) in South Carolina, USA. Bradley moved from South Carolina to Oakland city in the San Francisco Bay Area, where he also works.

He formed two Black indie bands, The Heist and The Accomplice, with three Ridge View High School's schoolmates. Bradley then earned a bachelor's degree in graphic design from the University of South Carolina in 2009. Afterward, he became friends with chillwave artist Ernest Greene (aka Washed Out).

9. Vagabon

Laetitia Tamko, alias Vagabon, was born and raised in Yaoundé, Cameroon. She is 29 years old as of 2021 because her birthday was 25th October 1992. The singer moved to New York at age 13 with her mum, for she needed to study law in the US.

Vagabon had to learn English, for she was from a French-speaking country. She attended Westchester High School and City College of New York. The lady also graduated from the Grove School of Engineering in 2015.

At age 17, her parents bought her a Fender acoustic guitar from Costco. She learned to play it using instructional DVDs and began uploading her music to Bandcamp in 2014 under a pseudonym, Vagabon.

10. Young Fathers

Kayus Bankole, Alloysious Massaquoi, and Graham 'G' Hastings formed the Young Fathers band in 2008 while they were still teenagers. The trio performed in nightclubs before signing up with LA-based label Anticon in 2012.

One of their first mixtapes received the SAY Award (the Scottish Album of the Year Award). The band later signed up with Big Dada and released their debut album, Dead, in 2014. The album was 35th on the UK albums chart, and it also received the prestigious Mercury Prize.
