25 most beautiful Swahili words &amp; phrases: Learn the Swahili language

June 25, 2024
blog

The most beautiful Swahili words roll off the tongue and carry the effect of the message in their intonation. In short, it is tough to speak Swahili without feeling the emotion in every word, not to mention the language's ease of talking. Beautiful words in Swahili are also easy to remember.

Swahili is an African language spoken mainly in Kenya, Tanzania and Uganda. However, speakers of this melodic, emotive language can be found across the African continent. The language is heavily influenced by Arabic. In fact, the word ‘Swahili’ itself is Arabic for ‘coastal people’. The flows and patterns of the Swahili language are strong reflections of Arabic influence.

List of the most beautiful Swahili words and phrases

Do you want to learn Swahili words? Swahili is a relatively simple language to learn, mostly because words are pronounced as they are written. If you wish to learn the basic words and phrases, you should check out these beautiful Swahili words and meanings.

1. Hujambo - How are you?

This is one of the most beautiful African words. 'Hujambo' is the Swahili word for ‘how are you?’ It is mostly used formally as opposed to 'Jambo' or 'Habari', which mean the same thing, more or less.

2. Nafurahi kukuona - Pleased to meet you

Nafurahi kukuona simply means "I’m pleased to meet you". It can also be used to mean "I'm so glad to see you," depending on whether the context is formal or casual.

3. Nakupenda - I love you

Nakupenda is arguably one of the most loving ways of saying "I love you" in Swahili. If you wish to emphasize the phrase to mean "I love you so much", just add the word ‘sana', which translates to 'a lot'.

4. Karibu nyumbani - Welcome home

'Karibu nyumbani' is Swahili for 'welcome home'. It is among the Swahili phrases for welcoming guests or even a loved one after a long day at the office or work. 'Karibu' means 'welcome' and 'nyumbani' means 'home'.

5. Wapendeza - You look lovely

'Wapendeza' is Swahili for 'you look lovely or beautiful'. Of all Swahili words that mean beautiful, 'wapendeza' is the most appealing as well as respectable of them all. When it comes to Swahili words, 'beautiful' has a few more terms. Other Swahili words for beautiful include ‘mrembo,’ and ‘nzuri.’

6. Samahani - Sorry

'Samahani' is Swahili for 'sorry'. Other beautiful words for sorry in Swahili include ‘pole’ and ‘niwie radhi.’ If the offense is severe, you can say "Samahani" then follow it up with "Niwie radhi" to emphasize your remorse.

7. Polepole - Slowly

'Polepole' is among the most beautiful words in Swahili for 'slowly'. This term is often used when instructing someone to slow their pace whether when driving, walking, or working on something.

8. Hakuna matata - No worries

'Hakuna matata' is among the beautiful Swahili phrases that are common in Western countries. Surprisingly, most people in East Africa hardly use the phrase, which means 'no worries', but if you tell them "Hakuna matata," they will understand what you mean.

9. Karibu - Welcome

If you do a favor for someone and they thank you in Swahili, you can reply by saying: "Karibu", meaning 'you’re welcome'. 'Karibu' can also be used when inviting someone into your house or office.

10. Asante - Thank you

'Asante' is another common and beautiful Swahili word meaning 'thank you'. If you wish to emphasize the gratitude, simply add 'sana' and say "Asante sana", meaning thank you so much.

11. Tuko pamoja - We are together

'Tuko pamoja' simply means 'we are together'. People around East Africa often use it when reassuring their support to their friends or colleagues and view it as a message of unity and solidarity.

12. Hata nikitenda wema - Even when I do good

'Hata nikitenda wema hamwachi kusema' is Swahili for ’even when I do good, you still talk ill.’ It is among the hundreds of beautiful Swahili quotes found on lesos or kangas.

13. Mapenzi mubashara - Love is beautiful

'Mapenzi mubashara' is Swahili for 'love is beautiful'. If speaking of the beautiful things love has to offer and you lack enough words, you can sum it up by saying, "Mapenzi mubashara".

14. Umoja ni nguvu utengano ni udhaifu - Unity is strength division is weakness

This Swahili phrase meaning 'unity is strength division is weakness' is very popular in East Africa. It is used by leaders to unify people as well as family members or colleagues when in disagreement.

15. Kila jambo na wakati wake - Everything has its time

Everything has its time; whatever is yours will not pass you. I bet you've heard that before. If you wish to advise someone regarding time management in Swahili, tell them "Kila jambo na wakati wake."

16. Asiyefunzwa na mamaye hufunzwa na ulimwengu - Whoever doesn't learn from what their mother tells them will learn from the world

This Swahili quote means ‘whoever doesn't learn from what their mother tells them will learn from the world.' In short, listen to your mother's advice or learn the hard way.

17. Adui mpende - Love your enemy

'Adui mpende' is Swahili for 'love your enemy'. To “love your enemy” you need to find it in your heart to put aside any wrongs, and to love them as a fellow human being.

18. Fuata maji yaendako - Go with the flow

Go with the flow translates to 'fuata maji yaendavyo' in Swahili. 'Fuata' means 'follow' while 'maji' and 'yaendavyo' mean 'water' and 'flow' respectively.

19. Fimbo ya mbali haiuwi nyoka - The rod that's far away does not kill the snake

This Swahili saying loosely translates to ‘the rod that's far away does not kill the snake.' In short, proximity is required in specific instances for success.

20. Siku njema huonekana asubuhi - A good day is seen in the morning

This Swahili saying means 'a good day is seen in the morning'. 'Asubuhi' is Swahili for 'morning' while 'siku' and 'njema' mean 'day' and 'good' respectively.

21. Upendo - Love

'Upendo' is one of the most famous Swahili words for love. :Love is a profoundly tender, passionate affection for another person. It is a feeling of warm personal attachment or deep affection for another person.

22. Yote yang’aayo si dhahabu - All that glitters is not gold

This is one of the most famous African words with deep meaning. It translates to 'all that glitters is not gold'.

23. Maji hayatokoti yakishushwa kwa moto - If water has been removed from the fire, it'll stop boiling

This phrase simply translates to 'if water has been removed from the fire, it'll stop boiling'.

24. Haba na haba hujaza kibaba - Little by little the pot is filled

This is one of the powerful Swahili words. It translates to little by little the pot is filled. The English saying that's related to this one is 'the journey of a thousand miles begins with one step.

25. Mtaka cha mvunguni sharti ainame - he/she who wants something kept under the bed must bend

The phrase directly translates to 'he/she who wants something kept under the bed must bend'. You can use this phrase to encourage your friends and family members to work hard since good things only come after making an effort.

There you have it;a comprehensive list of the most beautiful Swahili words. If you're planning a trip to East Africa, you should make an effort to learn these few basic phrases of Swahili before you go. Being able to converse with the locals in the Swahili language goes a long way towards bridging the cultural gap.
