Disabled teen traumatized after classmates mock him while using his wheelchair

March 06, 2024
blog

An outraged mother is calling for the bullies who left her disabled son traumatized  after stealing his wheelchair and making fun of him to face consequences. 

Kimberly Mitchell of Louisiana sent a letter addressing a recent 'senior prank' at Abbeville High School in which students mocked her ninth grader son Tay'Shawn Landry, who has cerebral palsy and requires a motorized wheelchair. 

In the videos, a group of youngsters are seen wreaking havoc by covering a hallway in toilet paper, stacking desks and chairs on top of each other and riding around in the wheelchair. 

One video shared by the mother shows a student in Landry's chair appearing to slur his speech and make squealing noises while another person recording laughs. 

In a post on Facebook, Mitchell said the group 'played with the wrong child and the wrong mama' and 'there will be consequences.' 

'Sr. Pranks are fun and all but when you make fun of my disabled kid and his belongings then IT BECOMES MY PROBLEM,' the angry mother wrote.

A mother is outraged and calling for bullies to face discipline after videos posted online show teens using her disabled son's wheelchair and making fun of him

Kimberly Mitchell of Louisiana sent a letter addressing a recent 'senior prank' at Abbeville High School in which students mocked her son Tay'Shawn Landry (pictured) who has cerebral palsy and requires a motorized wheelchair

In an interview with KLFY, Landry said the videos broke his heart and caused him to feel unwilling to return to school.

'I was upset. I was mad. I was crying. I tried to stop myself from crying because I wanted to go to school. [I] couldn't. I was just upset,' he said. 

Landry's wheelchair had been in a classroom at the time  the students entered the school, an official with the Vermilion Parish School System said. 

'On Friday, videos became available via social media that the students gained access into a locked room that housed an electric wheelchair on loan to an AHS student that uses it during the school day,' Superintendent Tommy Byler wrote.   

'Multiple students were seen riding through the halls on the wheelchair and displayed actions that were insensitive and disrespectful to students with disabilities,' he continued. 

The superintendent wrote that he and the other school board members are 'completely disappointed' and 'in no way condone' the behavior. 

Byler's statement comes days after the heartbroken and angry mother called out the bullies who had taken the opportunity to mock him while he wasn't around. 

'As a mother, it hurt me to see my son upset and not wanting to go back to school because he took it as people making fun of him because he's different,' she wrote.

'Step into his shoes and tell me how y'all would feel?' Mitchell said. 'Yes, I know y'all joke and clown with him on a daily basis. The difference is those things he's in talk presences, last night he wasn't.' 

Even more heartbreaking is that Landry said he was shocked and saddened by the videos and his fellow classmates' participation. 

'Some people that I go to school with and they want to turn their back on me and do this; that is not acceptable,' Landry said.

In a statement, Byler said the students who had participated in the videos had apologized to Landry. 

He also said conversations with the teen and the others had taken place. 

But Landry's family and friends remain furious after seeing the hurt the incident has caused him, on top of the pain and suffering he has lived with while growing up.  

'His ambulation is horrible,' Marilyn Mitchell, his grandmother, said. 

'He never stood. He had never walked in his life. He has been crawling, and then after the month of the surgery, he was trying to ambulate. Now he has scoliosis making it even harder for him to move around,' she continued. 

'Let them see how it is to be on that floor on that cement going back and forth. Just how they were riding and closing whatever. See that pain. See the agony. See the frustration of disability people,' the grandmother said. 

His other grandmother expressed her concern for his mental state following the horrific incident.  

'With everything that's going on in the world today with the kids, we don't know how it's going to affect him in the long term,' said Clarice Landry.  

'Where are the consequences for these children? His mother hasn't heard anything about the consequences that they're going to bring forth to these kids,' she added.

'Why did they allow this? We are taxpayers so the school system is allowing the kids to go into the school to do this and mock children with disabled,' she continued.

'They say that these are good kids. I'm not saying that they're not good kids, but if you are a good kid, why did you do this to Tay'Shawn? Why did y'all mock him like that?' the concerned grandmother told the outlet.

In a lengthy statement, Byler said the matter remains under investigation. 

The superintendent wrote that 'necessary disciplinary actions' could come from the results of the school's investigation. 

He added that any disciplinary actions against students or staff are confidential. 

'The Vermilion Parish School system does not condone any disrespect to students with disabilities nor any behaviors that include damage to school property in any manner,' his statement concluded.
