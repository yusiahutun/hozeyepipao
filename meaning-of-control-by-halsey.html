Meaning of Control by Halsey

March 09, 2024
blog

In Halsey's captivating song "Control," the artist delves into the complex theme of inner turmoil and the struggle for control over one's own mind. Throughout the lyrics, Halsey grapples with her own insecurities and confronts the dark forces that reside within her. The song is an unfiltered exploration of the artist's internal battles, capturing the emotions and thoughts that have haunted her.One standout lyric in the song is, "I'm meaner than my demons." This line reflects the artist's realization that she possesses a fierce strength within herself, capable of matching or even overpowering the negativity that resides within her. It suggests that despite the daunting internal conflicts she faces, she stands defiant and refuses to be defined or defeated by her demons.Another evocative line in the song is, "I'm bigger than these bones." Here, Halsey challenges the limitations imposed by her physical self, expressing a desire to transcend mere existence and explore the vast expanses of her own identity. It signifies a yearning for liberation and self-discovery beyond the constraints of her physical body, shedding light on the idea of finding one's true essence within the depths of their being.Additionally, the lyrics "And all the kids cried out, 'Please stop, you're scaring me'" serve as a powerful acknowledgment of the fears and anxieties that Halsey's internal struggles evoke in others. It highlights the way her turbulent emotions affect those around her, suggesting a deep-seated need for understanding and acceptance. This theme ties back to the overarching idea of control, as she grapples with the impact her darkness has on those she loves.The song also explores the concept of artistic expression as a means of catharsis and immortality. Halsey writes, "They beg me to write them, so they'll never die when I'm dead." This line signifies the artist's recognition that by giving voice to her internal villains through her music, she can immortalize them. It showcases the power of creativity in providing an outlet for emotions, as well as the desire to leave a lasting impact through her art.Ultimately, "Control" encompasses themes of self-discovery, fear, and the struggle for autonomy over one's thoughts and emotions. Halsey's raw and introspective lyrics paint a vivid portrait of her personal battles, while also evoking universal emotions that resonate with listeners. Through her honest exploration of these complex themes, she invites us to reflect on our own struggles with control and to find solace in the acknowledgment that we are not alone in our journey towards self-acceptance.
