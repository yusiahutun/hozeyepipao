Shabana FC Supporters Ungovernable as Club Collects KSh 318,000 In Tickets During Midday Match

August 04, 2024
blog
  • Fans filled Shabana FC's home turf, Gusii Stadium, as they played against National Soccer League (NSL) side Mully Children's Family FC
  • The home team did not disappoint the home fans by securing a 3-1 win and will need just a point to win the NSL title
  • The club, which got promoted to the Kenya Premier League after 17 years, collected KSh 318,000 at the gate

Shabana FC has announced that the club collected a considerable amount of money during their match against Mully Children's Family FC in the NSL.

Shabana FC gets overwhelming support

Despite being played at midday, fans filled the Gusii Stadium to watch the team win 3-1.

The home team scored in the 10th minute through Isaac Otieno before John Otieno equalised for the visitors.

However, second-half goals by Mustapha Oduor and Nehemiah Onchiri helped Shabana secure a convincing win.

Footage captured at the stadium showed chaotic scenes as the players celebrated their win by waving at the crowd, which went wild.

The club later announced that 2,820 fans attended the match.

“A total of 2,830 tickets were sold yesterday. VIP 350. Regular 2480Asante sana for all those who made it to Gusii Stadium for our match,” they wrote.

Shabana FC ticket sales, promotion

Afande, a young and renowned contractor who attended the match, disclosed that a VIP ticket went for KSh 200 while a regular ticket cost KSh 100. The ticket sales amounted to Ksh 318,000.

Ondanya, a blogger and part of the club’s communication team, expressed pride in his team, noting they received immense support.

“I feel so happy witnessing Shabana return to the FKF Kenya Premier League. I’d like to thank our fans for the outpouring of support, the players and our able coach Sammy Okoth.

Not forgetting Kisii county leaders led by our patron, Governor Simba Arati.”

The win meant that Shabana, whose motto is tore bobe (we’re on fire), must draw or win their next match to secure the NSL title.

Shabana earned a promotion to the KPL after beating Migori Youth in their NSL match on their home turf on Sunday, June 18.

It was a 17-year wait to return to top-flight Kenyan football after being relegated in 2006. Jubilant fans flooded the streets of Kisii town to celebrate the well-deserved promotion.
