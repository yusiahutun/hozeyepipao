Having blonde hair is really cool! But making your hair stay the same way can be quite a task as your precious locks are vulnerable to getting warm, brassy tones that give them an unflattering look. Regardless of how good your haircare routine is, you cannot quite prevent environmental factors, such as sun exposure from tarnishing your gorgeous hair color. Hence, the best toners for blonde hair are products to swear by, regardless of whether you have naturally bright tresses or have gotten them colored. 

Toners are essentially color correctors for your hair, which cancel out those dreaded warm tones on your hair with cool shades such as purple, blue, bluish-gray, and silver. These hues neutralize the ones placed opposite to them on the color wheel, thereby giving a clean, refreshed look to your hair. In addition, toners are also useful in enhancing your hair color and making it appear more vibrant. 

You would be happy to know that DIY blonde toning products, be it purple shampoos, sprays, hair masks, or toning drops give shockingly similar results to that of salon treatments while saving you precious bucks.     How to Select the Best Toner for Blonde Hair?

    Here are a few factors to consider before investing in a toner for blonde hair. 

    Formulation: Hair color toners are available in different kinds of formulations that vary based on methods of application, duration, lasting power, benefits, etc. If you are looking for a toner to simply touch up your hair and lightly improve shine, then a shampoo or leave-in spray can work adequately. However, to neutralize stubborn yellow or orange tones, you should get your hands on a highly pigmented hair mask or toning liquid to achieve the desired results.

    In addition to toning pigments, look for products with nourishing ingredients coconut oil, argan oil, aloe extracts, glycerin, sweet almond oil, panthenol, etc. to boost hydration in your hair. 

    Also, always ensure that the product you’re using is free from harmful contents such as phthalates, mineral oil, formaldehyde, ammonia, hydroquinone, and toluene as they can cause hair damage and hormonal problems. While this is difficult to find in many demi-permanent hair toners, a formula free from hydrogen peroxide makes it safer for your precious tresses. 

    Color Intensity of Your Hair: While all blonde toners consist of cool-toned colors to neutralize the brassiness of your hair, the precise color you need varies depending on your hair color. If your natural or pre-lightened hair has a very light and pale blonde color, then a purple or silver toner would be ideal for reducing the yellow tones on it. However, if your hair is medium, dark, or dirty blonde, then a blue or silver-blue toner would be perfect to neutralize the rusty, orange tones on it. The key is in selecting a pigment that is placed opposite to your hair color on the color wheel. 

    Hair Type: While most toners work well on all hair types, some formulations work only for certain hair types such as low porosity, curly, etc. Hence, always check whether the product packaging explicitly mentions so. 

    Lasting Power: The effects of an over-the-counter color toner usually last from 4 to 8 weeks, depending on the formulation used. So, understand what kind of results you are looking for and how often you would actually be able to use the product before making a purchase. 

    In addition to the above-mentioned factors, ensure that your toner does not bleed or deposit stubborn pigments on your hair. 

    How to Use a Toner for Blonde Hair?

    The methods to use a toner vary depending on the formula you use. Here are the ways to use a few common toner formulations:

    Creme + Developer: This is by far the most elaborate and long-lasting toner formula that needs to be applied through meticulous steps. To begin with, mix the color creme and developer in a 50:50 ratio. Then, depending on the viscosity of the toner, you can either use a dye brush to apply the product or pour the mixture directly on your hair. Ensure you wear rubber gloves when performing this task as the concentrated products can cause your fingers to stain. Cover your hair with a shower cap after application and rinse off after the instructed duration. 

    Hair Mask/Glaze: The second most long-lasting and concentrated formula in the list, a hair mask or glaze simply skips one step from the former method, as there is nothing to mix. And when you put on secure gloves on your hands, you don’t even have to use a dye brush to apply the product. Take a generous amount of the product in batches in your hands and apply it directly on the roots and lengths of your hair. Be careful at this step, as such products easily stain the walls and the floor. Let the product settle in your hair for 30-40 minutes or as instructed in the product manual. Then, rinse it off. 

    Toning Liquid: A toning liquid is the most versatile toner formulation as you can use it in multiple ways — directly or by mixing it with your shampoo or deep conditioner. However, the specifics of its usage vary based on the product, so ensure to read the instructions manual thoroughly. 

    Toning Shampoo: If the shampoo you’re using is sulfate-free and rich in emollients, then it would be a good idea to let the product settle for 5-15 minutes after lathering it on wet hair. For soap-based shampoos, avoid keeping them longer than 2-3 minutes as the sulfates can strip your hair and scalp of its natural moisture. Rinse off the product after use. 

    Leave-in Spray: Leave-in sprays are the easiest to use from the list. All you need to do is spritz a generous amount of the product after shampooing and towel-drying your hair. You don’t need to rinse them off and the formula gets plenty of time to gradually enhance your hair color. 

    Talking about the usage of a toner, our contributor Gwenda Harmon, says, “You may use a toner around four to eight weeks for damaged hair. This is advisable to achieve your desired shade or neutralizing fragmented tones. A regular permanent toner can be used for four to six weeks between two toning operations for regular permanent toning. After this, your hair will experience regrowth.”

    FAQ's

    What is the best toner for freshly bleached hair?

    For freshly bleached hair, it is best to avoid using any toner as over-exposure to chemicals can cause hair damage. The healthy way to use a toner is to wait for about two weeks after bleaching or pre-lightening your hair.

    Do toners damage bleached hair?

    A toner can cause damage to your bleached or pre-lightened hair only when you overuse it or use it less than a week after bleaching.
