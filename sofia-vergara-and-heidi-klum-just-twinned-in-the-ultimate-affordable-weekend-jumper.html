Sofia Vergara and Heidi Klum just twinned in the ultimate affordable weekend jumper

July 29, 2024
blog

Only powerhouses Sofia Vergara and Heidi Klum could make the simple art of “doing less” into the most iconic fashion statement.

The duo, who are both judges on America's Got Talent, wore identical sweatshirts which sported the phrase 'I will accomplish nothing today.' Heading into the long weekend, it is the motto we are choosing to live by.

The design hails from sustainable street wear brand Teddy Fresh, who pride themselves on “Making clothes that you can wear.” In an official statement the label explain, “We are focused on making high-quality products from better materials that promote a positive impact on both people's lives and the environment.”

The renown TV personalities took the twinning aesthetic a step further by both pairing their jumpers with thigh-high boots. The Modern Family star opted to wear hers with Opaque tights while Klum served “supermodel off duty” in black leather trousers. Thigh-high boots are a perennial wardrobe classic, even as we head into the depths of spring, “make no mistake - your footwear collection will be incomplete without them,” explains Hello! Fashion’s Orin Carlin, “From Julia Roberts' iconic black patent boots in Pretty Woman, to Michelle Obama's insanely dazzling Balenciaga pair – it's clear that everyone needs a pair of thigh-high boots in their footwear arsenal.”

The pair have become close pals thanks to their time spent together on the show, late last year Vergara appeared on fellow judge Howie Mandel’s podcast ‘Howie Mandel Does Stuff’ and discussed how the two are often compared to each other, "I feel like I'm secure. But imagine, being 50 and sitting next to a supermodel," she said. 

The Colombian 50-year-old and the German 49-year-old were already household names before they joined the AGT franchise. Vergara has appeared as a judge since season 15 (2020) and Heidi has been on the show since March 2013, when it was announced that she would be replacing Sharon Osbourne as a judge.
