New York woman 'EVICTED' from a McDonald's after restaurant gave 30 minute time limit

May 07, 2024
blog

A New York woman says she was asked to leave a McDonald's restaurant in New York after staff enforced a policy which restricts dine-in customers to a 30-minute time limit.

Jane Malin of Little Falls and a few friends were enjoying a post Sunday Mass meal at at their local chain when they were reportedly kicked out by employees.  

In a Facebook post shared on Monday, Malin explains the quick encounter. 

'In 60+ years I've never been thrown out of a public restaurant until yesterday. The local Little Falls, NY, McDonald's has a new policy to evict customers after 30 minutes.'

Jane Malin and a group of customers were kicked out of a Little Falls McDonald's after 34 minutes because the fast food chain enforced a 30 minute time limit 

'3 of us having coffee and lunch following Sunday Mass were asked to leave the restaurant after 34 minutes. Another table of 4 women were also evicted. The dining room was essentially cleared.'

Malin called the experience 'insulting', 'embarrassing' and 'ridiculous'. 

Initially, Malin though the employee who asked them to leave was kidding. 

It is unclear whether the 30 minute policy was enacted by McDonald's corporate or individual restaurant owners

She told Syracuse.com: 'We were just finishing our lunch. I was still drinking my large iced tea, and my friend was waiting for her coffee to cool down.'

'The employee was very polite, and we left but we couldn't believe it. I have been going there four to five times a week for several years, and we often go Sundays after Mass for lunch and to catch up,' she said. 

She reportedly told Syracuse.com that she believes they must have been timed, 'because they arrived at 12.08pm and asked to leave at 12.42pm.' 

WKTV reports that the Facebook post amassed nearly 1,000 shares and users in the comments were divided on McDonald's fast paced approach. 

WKTV reported one commentor saying: 'They have a big problem with kids hanging out in there and not buying stuff and going on their computers for free wi-fi.'

Another user said they were recently informed of the policy via phone call with a manager. 

'I was informed of the new policy the other night, via the phone handed to me by a waitress, with the manager on the other end... what a stupid move to drive away customers. 

'Many senior-citizens and retired people come to their local McDonalds to meet with friends and share a cup of coffee or a 'Happy' meal.'

It is not clear how long or if this policy will become regularly enforced in McDonald's restaurants.  

It is also not clear if McDonald's Corporation has mandated this policy or if it is at the discretion of owners.
