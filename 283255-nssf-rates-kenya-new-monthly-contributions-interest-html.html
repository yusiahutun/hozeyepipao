NSSF Kenya rates for monthly contributions (updated in 2024)

April 16, 2024
blog

The National Social Security Fund’s primary purpose is to provide social security protection to workers in the formal and informal sectors. The social fund registers members and receives regular contributions towards realising these objectives, including processing and paying out benefits to members and dependents. Recently, there have been approved changes to the NSSF Kenya rates. So, what are the latest NSSF rates applicable to all Kenyans?

Many people have been wondering about the changes in NSSF payments in February 2023. The National Social Security Funds effected this one-of-a-kind change in a move to collect more and avail more funds to its members upon retirement when they are due for payment. So, how does the contribution matrix look, and where do you fall?

How did the new NSSF Kenya rates come to be?

Do you regularly check your payslip? If you are keen, you would have noticed that you have a mandatory deduction every month labelled NSSF. And it was only a little before the new rates became effective as the employee and employer were required to submit to NSSF at least Ksh.200 monthly.

The NSSF contributions were, for a long time, governed by the National Social Security Fund Act, Cap 258 of the laws of Kenya of 1965. The Act stated that the social security fund would only deduct Ksh.200 to be paid by the employer and the employee.

However, some changes happened to the NSSF deduction rates on December 24, 2013, from suggested alterations to the National Social Security Fund Act. The Act also allowed voluntary contributions to the provident fund.

However, before it took effect, the Kenya Plantations & Agricultural Workers’ Union moved to court. Eventually, the concerned parties chose to sort the grey areas in the Act before making the changes active.

In 2023, the Court of Appeal allowed the government to activate the new contribution rates, thereby allowing the implementation of the new law that raised the contributions. And the law started working in February 2023.

The new NSSF payments with salary ranges

You probably ask yourself, “What are Tier 1 and 2 NSSF rates?” The question is quite common after people learnt that the new law divided contribution into Tier 1 and 2. Employers and employees in the Tier 1 group are meant to contribute Ksh.360 monthly, and those in Tier 2 should contribute Ksh.720.

According to the Act, the Upper Earning Limit (UEL) is Ksh.18,000, and the Lower Earning Limit (LEL) is Ksh.6,000. NSSF contribution rates comprise 12% of employees' pensionable salaries and wages.

NSSF monthly rates are divided into two equal parts: the employer pays 6%, and the employee pays the remaining 6%. The upper limit is increased annually until the fifth year, matching the national average earnings per the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics.

The contributions are pertinent to the salaries and wages that fail to meet a maximum of Kshs.720 of the Lower Earning Limit will be credited to the Tier I account. The excess amount for wages and salaries between the Upper Earning Limit and the Lower Earning Limit at a maximum of Kshs.1,440 will be credited to the Tier II account.

Besides the monthly payments, members can also top up their contributions. The top-up forms are available for download on the organization's website, and the guidelines are also simple and easy to follow.

Stipulations for the employer and the employees

The revised NSSF rates require a deduction of 6% of the worker's pensionable earnings. The employer should match this percentage for employed workers, making the total deduction 12% of the worker's earnings.

Below are the stipulations that the employer and the staff need to observe according to the new NSSF contribution rates:

  • The new act requires that all employers with more than one employee in their businesses immediately register as contributing employers with the NSSF.
  • Employers should ensure that all their workers are registered members of NSSF.
  • Late payment of the monthly contribution will attract a penalty of the entire monthly donation for every delayed month or the section of the month in which the employer or worker has failed to remit their contributions.
  • The workers should maintain updated and correct records, including their earnings and other relevant details.
  • Any employer or employee who fails to observe the new NSSF rates' stipulations will be considered to have committed a criminal offence. Therefore, they may be liable for prosecution.
  • Employees should provide accurate and current information for registration. They also should be aware of their social security rights. These are clearly stipulated in the NSSF guidebook, available for download on the website.

Thanks to technology, you can nowcalculate NSSF contributions and know the applicable rate from your phone's comfort. This knowledge helps to ensure that your employer is making the correct deductions. For voluntary contributors, the calculator helps them manage their funds effectively.

Users only need to enter their gross salary; the calculator will do the job. In case of further inquiries or clarification, you can always visit your nearest NSSF office or contact them on their social media platforms.

NSSF Kenya interest rates

The NSSF pension savings earn an interest of about 6.87% per year. This rate has been in effect since 2018. The payouts are credited to the depositors' accounts. The steady increase from previous years has seen more retirees enjoy their post-employment years.

The NSSF Kenya interest rates rise results from the organization's investment income. The fund has seen an increased flow of dividends from investments in companies like BAT and Safaricom. The organization has also been investing in government treasury bonds.

What is the NSSF payment deadline?

When are NSSF payments due? Every month's payments towards the National Social Security Fund are due before the 9th of the month. All payments made after the deadline elapses attract a penalty.

It is an employer’s responsibility to ascertain that they remit the NSSF payments on time. The employer should also ascertain that all employees are registered members and maintain proper records. It is also the employer’s role to make sure that all employees are aware of the new NSSF rates for employees.

Is it mandatory to remit the NSSF rates?

Section 18 of the NSSF Act of 2013 states that everyone subject to employment in Kenya and over 18 years should be a member of the Pension Fund. On top of this, employers must deduct the contribution, based on the prevailing NSSF monthly rates, from employees’ salaries.
