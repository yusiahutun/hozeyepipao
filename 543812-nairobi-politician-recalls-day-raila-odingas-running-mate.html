Nairobi gubernatorial aspirant in the 2022 elections, Agnes Kagure, has disclosed that she was once considered Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition presidential candidate Raila Odinga's running mate.

Nairobi 2022 gubernatorial race

Kagure contested as an independent candidate for the coveted Nairobi seat after her Jubilee Party of Kenya party and Raila's ODM parties agreed to field a joint candidate in the city.

Jubilee and ODM, the majority components of Azimio la Umoja One Kenya Coalition, alongside Kalonzo Musyoka's Wiper Democratic Movement, fielded Polycarp Igathe as their gubernatorial candidate and former Kibwezi MP Philip Kaloki as his running mate.

Azimio la Umoja lost the contest to Kenya Kwanza's Johnson Sakaja, who contested on a United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party.

While recalling how the 2022 county and national elections shaped up, Kagure disclosed that she was among the Azimio la Umoja politicians considered Raila's running mate in the presidential contest.

Earlier, she shared what, together with other gubernatorial aspirants, they went through within their parties and the main contest during the 2022 polls.

Raila, Agnes Kagure presidential ticket

"In the run-up to 2022, we were discussing the possibility of being Baba's running mate. Mnaona mambo ingeendaje hapo? (How would things have turned out?" She posed on her Facebook.

When probed into what the discussions entailed, Kagure opened up about how the coalition sought to leverage her passion for youth and women's empowerment to add value to their national agenda.

"Naturally, I am passionate about youth and women, as you can see in many of the development and charity programmes by my foundation. The discussions were inclined towards rallying women and youthful voters to our side," Kagure said.

Who is Kenya University Students Organisation patron?

Kagure, also the Kenya University Students Organisation (KUSO) patron, recalled how her passion for empowering the youth and women made her recognised among national leaders who could occupy the deputy president's office.

Kagure has built a vast network and strong support base among the youth and women through her empowerment programmes in the city and across the country.

Kenyans on Facebook gave their views about how Kagure and Raila's partnership would have fared.

Solomon Theorem stated the duo would have been a good pick for the youth.

"The duo could have empowered youths and created more job opportunities. Kenyans always go for the worst while ignoring the best," observed Theorem.

James Jane opined that the duo of Raila and Kagure could have outweighed Ruto and his deputy, Rigathi Gachagau.

"Absolutely, it could be more fire. We could not have lost that seat, but this is a bright future ahead for a successful team," James said.

Justus Kaimanya said that the team would have been stronger and urged Kagure to consider the partnership in 2027.

Raila Odinga chooses Martha Karua as running mate

"Mambo yamekuwa POA sana and try again in 2027 (things would have been so nice, try again in 2027)," Kaimanya said.Mercy Otieno: "The two would have made Nairobi a haven of opportunities for young people."

Azimio la Umoja leader Raila Odinga settled for NARC Kenya party leader, former Gichugu MP, and Water Minister Martha Karua as his running mate.
