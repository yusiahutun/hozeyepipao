Chynna Phillips is from a musical family

Music has been a significant part of Chynna’s, 56, life. Her parents are John and Michelle Phillips of the legendary band The Mamas & the Papas, making Chynna part of musical royalty.

But her true rise to fame came when she joined Wilson Phillips, a pop music trio formed with Carnie and Wendy Wilson, daughters of Brian Wilson of The Beach Boys.

The group’s self-titled debut album, released in 1990, was a commercial success, selling millions of copies worldwide. However, they went their separate ways in 1993, though sometimes reuniting over the years. Afterwards, Phillips pursued a solo career, releasing the album Naked and Sacred in 1995. She later collaborated with singer-songwriter Vaughan Penn in the duo Chynna & Vaughan.

She has also appeared in films

Phillips later dabbled in film, appearing in Some Kind of Wonderful (1987) and Say Anything … (1989). Her credits also include appearances in Caddyshack II (1988) and the hit comedy Bridesmaids (2011), where she played herself. Additionally, she voiced the character Kitty in the animated series Danny Phantom.

She’s been married to Billy Baldwin for 29 Years

Baldwin and Phillips’, 61, love story began with a meet-cute in 1991. The pair met before they boarded a plane to New York, and Baldwin ultimately asked her out by the baggage claim. Their life together, marked by the birth of their three children – Jameson, Vance and Brooke – has faced challenges, notably a brief divorce filing by Phillips in 2010. But she quickly withdrew the filing, admitting to seeking more depth in their marriage, per Us Weekly.

Years later, the couple reportedly confronted and openly discussed a significant six-month separation, alongside issues such as Phillips’ growing faith. They have managed these difficulties through therapy and open communication, reaffirming their commitment to each other.

She is a Christian

After converting to Christianity in 2022, Phillips has candidly discussed the impact of her faith on her personal life, particularly how it has affected her marriage and friendships. On her YouTube channel, California Preachin’ – after the famous Mamas & the Papas song “California Dreamin’” – she shared the deep pain she’s felt over friends, family members and colleagues who don’t share her religious beliefs and are “not saved”.

Despite her attempts to share her faith and even baptise a few friends, many have not been receptive, leaving her feeling “gutted” over their reactions.
