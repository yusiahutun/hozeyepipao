Scott Peterson is seen in rare photo from video call with his family from prison

March 23, 2024
blog

Scott Peterson has been spotted smiling in a rare video call with his family from prison - as he files a new appeal over his 2004 conviction for the murder of his wife and their unborn child. 

Peterson killed Laci Peterson, 27, and their unborn son, Conner, on Christmas Eve 2002 - and he has been in prison for 19 years.  

The new image from the video call on May 22, 2022, obtained by Fox News, shows the convicted murderer grinning ear to ear during a prison conference call.  

Janey Peterson, his sister-in-law, said that he was sitting in a cell wearing a gray shirt and prison uniform, but the virtual background made it look as if he was sitting by a window near some trees. 

In October, a judge removed him from death row during allegations of a juror bias when he was convicted nearly two decades ago. He's now fighting his conviction.

He was on a call with his relatives - including his brother Joe Peterson, and sister-in-law Janey, his sister Susan and her husband Ed Caudillo.

Lee Peterson, his father, was also on the call. Caudillo was trying on blue overalls that the family had purchased for Scott in prison, according to Fox. 

Despite pushing to have a phone call with the convict once a week, the family said that they usually are able to video chat with him once or twice a month because of prison limitations. 

The new image comes amid a new development in his case. According to Fox, Peterson has filed a new 800-page petition of habeas corpus in a California appellate court.

It allegedly argues that his family has found new evidence that proves his innocence, but the document, filed on April 19, has not been made public. 

In August 2020, California's Supreme Court overturned his death sentence, and in 2022 he was move to Mule Creek State Prison, east of Sacramento.   

He was resentenced to life without parole but was denied a new trial. 

In December 2022, Judge Anne-Christine Massullo ruled that Peterson will remain behind bars for the rest of his life, after his request for a new trial on the grounds that his conviction was compromised by a biased juror was tossed out. 

He was convicted in 2004.

Peterson's attorney called the ruling 'disappointing' in a statement to Fox News Digital at the time: 'Just in the last few months, we have learned new information that will prove Scott Peterson did not murder his wife, Laci.
