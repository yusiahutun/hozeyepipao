Flags are symbolic not just because of the designs but also because they have a history. Different countries have different flag designs. This is influenced by their region, culture, beliefs, religion and love for their country. However, there are colours considered dominant in most flags, and the colours red and white are among the key combinations. Countries with red and white on their flags almost always share the same symbolism. The red often stands for the blood spilt during the quest for liberation, while the white colour represents peace and purity in most flags.

Reading about flags is fascinating as it tells the story of where people come from and their ambitions. A flag gives citizens an identity. Every national red and white national flag is symbolic and should be respected. Many countries fought for the freedom they enjoy, which is why the world's red and white flags are to be valued and celebrated.

Countries with red and white on their flags

Every country on the planet has its national flag. Each flag comes in many colours and is illustrated with geometric features and symbols. They are often tied to the country's history and culture. So, which countries around the globe only have the colours red and white on their flag? They are as follows:

1. Austria

Austria is one of the countries with a national flag that is red and white. The flag of Austria has three bands, the top and bottom being red, while the middle is white. Austria adopted the flag in 1230. Austria's flag is one of the oldest used by a modern country. The red bands symbolize strength and bravery, while the white represents peace and honesty.

2. Bahrain

The national flag of Bahrain has a white band on the left, separated from a red area on the right by five triangles that serve as a jagged line. Bahrain, a Muslim majority country, had to have some Islam representation on the flag.

The triangles that appear as an uneven line symbolize the five pillars of Islam, while the red represents the Kharijite sect of the Islamic religion. Bahrain officially adopted the flag on 14th February 2002.

3. Canada

The Canadian flag is a spectacle. It consists of a vertical triband of red on the hoist side and white colour at the centre. The red maple leaf on top of the field is visually appealing. Vertical red bands on the flag stand for Canadian culture, hope, peace and neutrality, while white represents the impartiality of the nation.

The maple leaf symbolizes cultural heritage. Canadians officially adopted the flag on 15th February 1965, and they adore their flag so much that they celebrate National Flag day on 15th February every year.

4. Denmark

The flag of Denmark has a red field with a white Nordic cross slightly positioned to the hoist side. Danes call the flag Dannebrog and have been using it since the 13th century. The white colour on the flag represents Christianity, while the red background pays homage to those who went to battle and bled for their country.

5. Greenland

The flag of Greenland has a captivating design, having two equal horizontal bands of white at the top and red at the bottom. Centred in is a large disk slightly to the hoist side. The disk has the colours red at the top and white at the bottom.

This flag was adopted on 1st May 1989. The white part of the band represents the ice cap, and the red part of the band stands for the ocean. The red part of the disc is a symbol of the icebergs and the pack ice, while the red half of the disk represents Greenland fjords.

6. Indonesia

The Republic of Indonesia is another exciting country that has the colours red and white on its flag. Indonesians call the flag 'Sang Saka Merah Putih' as it has a lofty bicolour of red and white. The colours on the flag represent human blood in red and the human spirit in white. The flag was adopted on 17th August 1945

7. Japan

The Japanese flag is one of the world's most recognizable red and white flags, only consisting of a rectangular white banner with a crimson-red disk at its centre. Japanese people refer to their flag as 'Hinomaru'. The white colour is symbolic of the sun, while the white colour represents the purity and integrity of the Japanese. The current flag was adopted on 13th August 1999.

8. Latvia

Latvia is located on the Baltic Sea. Its flag has a reddish-maroon colour bisected by a narrow white stripe. The flag of Latvia was adopted on 18th November 1918. The white bar on the flag represents peace and independence, while the red stand for the blood spilt by Latvians as they defended their sovereignty.

9. Malta

Malta is an archipelago between Sicily and the North African coast in the central Mediterranean. The flag has two colours; white on the hoist and red on the fly. On the white side is the George Cross, a cross given to Maltese people by George VI in 1942. The white symbolises peace, love, and light, while the red represents the sacrifice of the Maltin people as they defended their faith.

10. Monaco

Monaco is located on the northern coast of the Mediterranean Sea. Monaco's flag is simple, only having two equal bands at the top and bottom. The top band is red, while the base is white. The red colour represents the human body's flesh, while white symbolizes purity in spiritual life. The country has been using its current flag since 4th April 1881.

11. Peru

Any country with a vertical red and white flag? Peru. The South American country has a fascinating flag, with a vertical triband of red on the hoist side and fly side, and the colour white in the middle. On top of the white is the coat of arms. Like on many other flags, white symbolises peace and purity, while white represents the blood spilt on the quest for liberation.

12. Poland

The Republic of Poland is located in central Europe. It is one of the many countries with flags that have red and white colours. The Polish flag has two equal horizontal bands of red bottom and white (top).

Polish folk officially adopted their flag on 9th July 1807. The white symbolises freedom and purity, while the red pays homage to Polish patriots in their quest for independence.

13. Singapore

Singapore is located in southeast Asia and is known as one of the most hospitable countries in the region. It is among nations with a horizontal red and white flag. The flag of Singapore consists of a horizontal bicolour of red and white bands on top and bottom, respectively.

A white crescent moon with five stars on the red bar is overlaid on the right. The red is for universal brotherhood and equality of man, while white stands for purity. Singapore adopted the flag on 3rd December 1959.

14. Switzerland

Switzerland is known for being mountainous. The central European nation has a flag similar to the red cross flag. Interesting fact; both flags originate from the same country. While the Swiss flag has a white cross on top of a red background, the red cross has a red cross on a white field.

The design of the Red Cross flag was initiated in the First Geneva Convention in 1864. The symbol embodies an inverted Swiss flag to respect Henry Dunant, the Swiss founder of the International Committee of the Red Cross.

The colours on the Flag of Switzerland initially stood for freedom, honour, and loyalty. In the modern-day, however, Swiss folk take pride in saying that their flag stands for neutrality, democracy, and peace, given the country's stature as a neutral country.

15. Tonga

The Oceania country is renowned for its whale swimming experiences. The Tonga flag is among the dozens of flags that adopted the red and white colours. The design of the flag consists of a red field with a white canton charged with a red couped cross.

The red on the flag represents Christianity, given Tonga people are 97% Christians. The white colour stands for purity. Tonga has been using its flag since 1875.

16. Tunisia

The north African country has one of those common flags with a star and the crescent moon. The Tunisian flag consists of A red field with a centred white circle enclosing a red five-pointed star surrounded by a red crescent.

The sun disk symbolizes the radiance of Tunisia, and the crescent moon is symbolic of the unity of Muslims and the five pillars of Islam, while the white colour stands for peace. The red is to remember Tunisian men that fought the Ottoman empire. Tunisia adopted its flag in 1831.

17. Turkey

The flag of Turkey has a red background, a white five-pointed star, and a crescent moon on top of the red. The colours on the flag symbolize the reflection of the moon and a star in a pool of blood in Turkish legends. The star is also insignia for Turks. Turkish people have been using the flag since 1844.

18. England

The flag of England is designed with a white field with a centred red cross. It is derived from Saint George's Cross. The flag has been used since the 90s on various occasions such as in the Church of England, sporting activities, the Queen's 90th birthday celebration and English nationalism, among others.

19. Georgia

The flag of Georgia is also called the five-cross flag due to its design. It is a white field with a centred red cross and a red Bolnur-Katskhuri cross centre each quarter. Initially, this flag was used as a banner of the medieval Kingdom of Georgia before being re-popularized in the later 20th and early 21st centuries. It was adopted on 14th January 2004.

20. Gibraltar

Gibraltar is a British Overseas Territory and headland on Spain's south coast. It's dominated by the Rock of Gibraltar, a 426m-high limestone ridge. Its flag is a long banner of arms based on the coat of arms of Gibraltar, granted by Royal Warrant from Queen Isabella I of Castile on 10th July 1502 and adopted on 8th November 1982.
