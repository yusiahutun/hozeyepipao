    What is Low Porosity Hair?

    People with low porosity hair have a tight outer layer on their strands that are resistant to moisture. The cuticles are very tight and they don't allow water in easily. This may allow product buildup to occur while also not allowing conditioners to get fully absorbed. Individuals with this hair type struggle a lot with their hair being dry, becaus moisture never gets locked in. In this case, warm water can open up the cuticles a biy more, allowing lightweight moisture to seep in.

    What Causes Low Porosity Hair?

    There are a few reasons why you may have low porosity hair, the following are some of them:

    1. It may be genetical and inherited from your family.

    2. Tight curls that make moisture harder to get in.

    3. Overuse of heat and chemicals on the hair.

    4. Product buildup, especially silicones.

    5. Frequent use of hard water.

    How to Know if You Have Low Porosity Hair?

    The following are a few ways to check your hair porosity. Check for these signs:

    1. Water and haircare prodcuts tend to sit on your hair instead of getting absorbed.

    2. Put your hair into a glass of water and check to see if it floats or sinks. If it floats, it's liekly low porosity.

    3. If your fingers slide easily along strands of hair all the way from root to tip, then it may be because of low porosity.

    4. If you've noticed that your hair struggles in humid conditions, this could be another sign of low porosity.

    5. Low porosity hair is known to resist chemical treatments while also being less adaptive to heat styling.

    What to Look For When Buying the Best Low Porosity Hair Products?

    There are a number of important aspects to take into account when purchasing the best low porosity hair products to make sure you select the best options for your hair type. Here are some critical indicators to watch out for:

    1. Moisture Absorbent Elements 

    Hair with low porosity typically has a hard time absorbing moisture. Look for items that are specially designed to offer moisture retention and deep hydration. Low porosity hair benefits from key ingredients like glycerin, aloe vera, and humectants like honey or hyaluronic acid for drawing and holding onto moisture. Fatty acids also work great to retain the moisture of your locks. 

    2. Presence of Protein

    Protein helps to strengthen the hair, but too much protein can make low-porosity hair stiff and brittle. To keep your hair's elasticity and strength without overdosing on it, look for solutions with a balanced level of protein. For hair with low porosity, hydrolyzed proteins or amino acids are advantageous.

    3. Ingredients Present 

    Avoid products with harsh chemicals like sulfates, parabens, mineral oil, and others that can rob your hair of its own oils and make it even drier. Choose goods with healthy and natural components as opposed to those that include these substances. Try to look for moisture milk as an ingredient to retain hydration.  Sweet almond oil, coconut oil and olive oil are great ingredients to promote high-quality hair fibre and healthy hair. 

    4. pH Balance Ability 

    Hair with little porosity frequently has a pH level that is more alkaline, which can cause dryness and frizz. To assist in restoring and preserving the ideal pH balance of your hair, use items that are acidity-balanced or slightly acidic. Look for chemicals that can help maintain the pH balance of your hair, such as citric acid or apple cider vinegar.

    5. Feel on the Hair

    Avoid using products that are oily or heavy since they might weigh down your hair and produce buildup. Look for solutions that are light and won't make your hair feel greasy or thick. Argan oil and jojoba oil are both fantastic choices for providing hydration to your hair without weighing it down.

    What Are Some Ways to Care for Low Porosity Hair?

    Low-porosity hair needs special care to guarantee the best possible retention of moisture and hair quality. The following are crucial pointers for taking care of low-porosity hair as per our expertise: 

    Sharing a few hacks to cater to low porosity hair, our contributor Anna Misztela, says, “The problem with low porosity hair is that the hair cuticles are so tightly packed around the hair shaft, it is difficult for the shaft to be properly moisturized. The penetration of water is difficult. The result can be dehydrated hair and frizzy ends. Therefore, it is suggested to use products with lightweight oils that will have a better chance of penetrating through the tightly packed hair cuticles. Almond and argan oils are two good examples of lightweight oils. And for the same reasons, you would want to avoid products with butters and creams. To try to open up the cuticles a bit more, you can apply heat to your hair with warm water prior to applying a deep conditioner. And if you can, use steam. It is even more effective than warm water and can increase the effectiveness of your conditioning and moisturizing products. Finally, you can always try to moisturize your hair from the inside, so be sure to drink plenty of water.”

    1. Conditioning of Hair 

    Low porosity hair needs frequent deep conditioning procedures. Select deep conditioners designed especially for hair with low porosity, and use heat throughout the treatment to aid in opening the hair cuticles and facilitating efficient conditioner penetration. Add a hair steamer to this routine and you’ll see a significant change in hair loss. 

    2. Usage of Lukewarm Water

    When washing your hair, use tepid or lukewarm water rather than boiling water. The hair cuticles can be raised by hot water, which makes it harder for moisture to enter the hair shaft. To protect the cuticles and improve gloss, rinse with lukewarm water after washing.

    3. Gentle Cleaning 

    Prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils by using sulfate-free shampoos or co-washing, which involves using a conditioner to rinse your hair. Harsh cleaners may additionally impede moisture absorption while rendering low-porosity hair feel dry.

    4. Moisturizing 

    Lightweight moisturizers that can efficiently permeate the hair shaft are required for low-porosity hair. To lock in the moisture, use a mild oil or butter after applying water-based moisturizers or leave-in conditioners to damp hair. Low porosity hair can be moisturized using the "L.O.C." (liquid, oil, cream) or "L.C.O." (liquid, cream, oil) approaches.

    5. Deep Cleansing 

    Low-porosity hair is more susceptible to accumulation and can easily become weighted down. Use products minimally, and stay away from heavy butter and oils that could sit on the surface of your hair. Cleanse your hair every so often to get rid of product buildup and residue.

    FAQ's

    What products work best with low porosity hair?

    Moisturizing shampoos, deep-conditioners or leave-in conditioners, hair oil, and gels work best for low porosity hair types.

    What is the best ingredient for low porosity hair?

    Some of the best ingredients for low porosity hair are shea butter, rose water, coconut, tea tree, argan oil, honey, and spearmint. These shampoos thoroughly wash hair while gently eliminating all buildup and fortifying the hair to prevent breakage.

    What not to use on low porosity hair?

    Remember to not use heavy oils and silicon-based products as they create barriers and coatings on the strands, making it difficult for the hair to retain moisture.

    Which serum is good for low porosity hair?

    Look out for hair serums that contain the nutritional benefits of argan oil, jojoba oil, and grapeseed oil.

    Is henna good for low porosity hair?

    Hair that is particularly absorbent or highly porous responds well to henna. Hair with low porosity could not accept the color well.
