What Is Cheese Tea? - The Cheese Tea Trend Has Us Scratching Our Heads

August 05, 2024
blog

How do you take your tea? With cream? Sugar? How about some cheese? That's right: Cheese tea is apparently the latest food craze. Also known asHeytea, the cheese tea trend started in Taiwan and China, says the Washington Post—and now, you can officially get it in the U.S., too.

Gilmore Girls fans might recall that time Rory accidentally ordered cheese instead of cream with her coffee in Italian. But this is no accident. It's probably not exactly what you're picturing, either. No, people aren't swapping sugar cubes for cheddar cubes. And don't expect to see strangers shredding Swiss into their Starbucks orders.

So what is cheese tea? Basically, it's your brew of choice (whether black, green, oolong, jasmine, or even chocolate) topped off with a frothy mixture of sweet or salty cream cheese and condensed milk, the Post reports. It actually sounds a lot like a latte-meets-bubble tea, and dare we say, a bit delicious?

Maybe it's not so strange. After all, people have also been known to add butter or salt to coffee to cut the bitterness. Besides, science suggests there may be health benefits to both tea and cheese, so that's something, right?

Want to try it for yourself? You can now get cheese tea at Little Fluffy Head in Los Angeles and HappyLemon, which has locations in Boston, New York, and San Francisco. As the fad catches on, check to see if cafes in your area add it to their menus. But be prepared to wait in line.
