A-level students hit infamous nightclub strip for booze-fuelled night celebrating results

June 24, 2024

A-level students have flocked to the streets for some heavy drinking - be that to down their sorrows or success.

Results day is a big deal for students, with the grades inside the terrifying paper envelope carrying the power to potentially change the path of their future.

Up and down the country, young adults flooded to their local bars and clubs to celebrate the end of their school years and the beginning of the next stages of their lives.

One infamous spot that was particularly active on results night was Birmingham's well-trodden Broad Street, packed with bars clubs and kebab shops.

Images have surfaced of young people heading out and indulging in heavy nights out - often ending in friend's arms on a curb, well past midnight.

Grades this year are reportedly lower than they have been for the previous two years, which were heavily impacted by the pandemic, but are higher than pre-Covid levels.

Overall the number of top grades are understood to have dropped, yet the number of students being offered places at universities has approached breaking the record.

The number of grades ranked A* or A has fallen to 36.4% from 44.8% in 2021.
