Wes Anderson Gallery Opening in New York

June 12, 2024
blog

Throughout his career, director Wes Anderson has become as well known for his cinematic style as he is for the characters he creates through interesting fashion. Watching a Wes Anderson film is like stepping into a different world—with a slightly off-kilter pastel color palette, soundtracks from the past, and intricate interior details. It's a mix of old school glam and intellect with complex, compelling dialogues. His aesthetic is so strong that it's become the new norm to hear people dub things from other métiers (fashion, home design, art) as "Wes Anderson style."

Right now in particular, the director is having a major cultural moment. When Alessandro Michele presented his first collection for Gucci in February, it was widely considered to show the influence of Wes Anderson. This weekend only in New York City, a Wes Anderson pop-up exhibition is set to open. The exhibition will show fan art from over 60 different artists. Proving that Wes Anderson fans are seriously devoted, the Facebook event page already boasts 59k people who have RSVPed "yes". Here, a look at some of the director's best sartorial moments on screen.

1) Margot Tenenbaum in The Royal Tenenbaums: The first and very obvious choice in terms of fashion in Wes Anderson films is Margot from The Royal Tenenebaums. Played by Gwyneth Paltrow, Margot is a mix of vintage bombshell and uninspired prepster. The oversize mink fur coat (which happened to be made by Fendi), sheer nightie and loafers in contrast to her blond blunt cut bob, pinned neatly with a single barrette and striped Lacoste polos are precisely what make her so interesting (and the subject of many a Halloween party costume) as a style icon. As Gucci's Fall 2015 collection walked in Milan, the drooping furs, penny loafers and skin exposure were undeniably Margot-ish.

2) Eleanor Zissou in The Life Aquatic: Played by the striking former model and actress Anjelica Huston, Eleanor Zissou's presence is powerful in The Life Aquatic. Opposite Bill Murray, who wears a tuxedo and red beanie in one scene, Eleanor sports an asymmetrical grey gown and oversized green necklace—part tribal, part 60s alien chic. In another scene, it's her simple white button-down covered in strings of turquoise necklaces in addition to bracelets and rings that inspires. She's committed to power dressing with her own unique twist. And who could forget the scene where she lay smoking a cigarette in bed with a kitten on her lap—wearing a simple tube dress and pendant necklace.

3) Suzy Bishop in Moonrise Kingdom: In Moonrise Kingdom, Suzy Bishop's look is one 12-year-old's wardrobe we wouldn't mind stealing. Played by newcomer Kara Hayward, Suzy's look is a combination of 60s mod and schoolgirl. Her pastel colored peter pan dresses—always long-sleeved and rendered in lemon yellow and blush pink—are paired with knee-high socks and saddle shoes. On screen, Suzy's style and character are amped up with vintage suitcases, binoculars worn around the neck and books in hand. In real life—her style looks like it came straight off the runway as a sort of ode to geek chic.

4) Agatha in The Grand Budapest Hotel: Agatha's look is complex in The Grand Budapest Hotel. Played by Saoirse Ronan, her facial birthmark and milkmaid braids pay tribute to the unconventional beauty seen in much of Wes Anderson's work. When working in the bakery, her dusty powder blue shift, trimmed in pink, creates a sort of fashion nostalgia for all things related to vintage uniforms. Topped with a simple camel coat and armed with many small pink boxes filled with pastries and cakes throughout the film, Agatha ends up being a chic icon in her own way. It's no wonder Milena Canonero took home an Oscar for the film's costume design.

5) Jack's ex in Hotel Chevalier: Starring as Jack's ex in Hotel Chevalier, Natalie Portman is a vision in yellow. Created as a prologue to The Darjeeling Limited, the short film runs 13 minutes long. In one scene, Portman sits in bed—yellow walls, yellow bedframe, yellow blanket and yellow bathroom and a Bloody Mary bedside. Later, she stands on the hotel terrace wearing the same bright yellow bathrobe opposite Jack (Jason Schwartzman). The yellow-on-yellow lounging around in a bathrobe makes the possibility of monochromatic dressing seem extra chic—while the specific hue of bold yellow is a reference to the somewhat off-putting color palette we love the director for.

6) Rita in The Darjeeling Limited: There's something endlessly chic about Rita's (played by Amara Karan) traditional Indian costume in The Darjeeling Limited. Whether it's the bold turquoise and cerulean hues of her sari, the contrast invested in the fact the other three main characters wear Western clothing, or her thick rimmed glasses—her look becomes an irreplaceable component of the film.

The Wes Anderson Art Show is on view from August 7th-9th at Joseph Gross Gallery, 548 W 28th St. New York, New York.
