Tom Arnold is an actor and comedian from the United States. Tom Arnold is widely recognized for playing Arnie Thomas on Roseanne (1989–1993), Jackie Thomas on The Jackie Thomas Show (1992–1993), Tom Graham on Tom (1994), and Tom Amross on The Tom Show (1997–1998).

Early Life

Thomas Duane Arnold, known as Tom Arnold, was born on March 6, 1959, in the United States of America in Ottumwa, Iowa. As of now, he is 65 years old and has a birth sign of Pisces. He is American and belongs to a mixed ethnic background of English, Swedish, German, Irish, and Scottish.

He was born to his father Jack Arnold and his mother Linda Kay. Meanwhile, he grew up with his six siblings and was raised by his father’s single handle after his mother abandoned the family. He had a difficult childhood, as he has revealed that he was sexually abused by a male babysitter from the ages of four to seven.

Tom Arnold – Education

Moving on to his academic background, he attended Ottumwa High School. Later, he enrolled at the Indian Hills Community College. From 1981 to 1983, he attended the University of Iowa where he studied business administration and writing.

Professional Career

Tom Arnold moved to Minneapolis in the early 1980s and began performing his act “Tom Arnold and the Fabulous Goldfish Revue” at local comedy clubs. During this time, he met rising comedienne Roseanne Barr.

He made an appearance in the 1991 film “Freddy’s Dead: The Final Nightmare.” In addition, he played Howard Peterman in the 1991 TV movie “Backfield in Motion.” He made an appearance in the 1992 film “A Different World.”

Similarly, in 1993, he appeared in “The Woman Who Loved Elvis,” “Coneheads,” “Body Bags,” “The Larry Sanders Show,” and “Undercover Blues.” He has since appeared in several other films and television series. Moreover, he currently has more than 150 acting credits.
