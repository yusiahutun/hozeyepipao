Dave Burd, in full, David Andrew Burd, is a 35 years old young comedian who goes by the stage name – Lil Dicky. He is an actor, rapper, and comedian who rose to prominence by releasing the music video called Ex Boyfriend in 2013. With the release of the music, he reached the hearts of more than one million people on YouTube within just 24 hours of the release. He had his debut with the album called Professional Rapper in 2015.

Early Life

Dave Burd was born on March 15th 1988 in Cheltenham Township and was nurtured in an upper-middle-class Jewish family. Since childhood, he has been keenly interested in making music and listening to hip hops and alternative rock music. He was only in the 5th grade when he began using rap music after studying a history report on Alexander Pushkin. Dave would probably speak the chapter using his rapping skills. He would mostly listen to Nas and Jay-Z in his youth.

Dave went to Cheltenham High School, University of Richmond, and was a fellow of 2016 XXL Freshman and rapper Dave East in college. After receiving his college degree, he went to San Francisco, California, where he began working as an account management executive at the advertising agency Goodby, Silverstein & Partners. After seeing his continuous progress report, the company brought him to work in the creative department, where he would do the ad copies and write about things like the NBA’s “BIG” campaign.

Dave Burd Career

Dave Burd began with his first debut mixtape, “So Hard,” released in 2011. It took him nearly two years to complete the mixtape because he would spare his time for his day job with the previous company Goodby, Silverstein & Partners. Then in 2013, he released one song weekly for five consecutive months.

Soon after, in 2014, Burd had his live concert at TLA in Philadelphia on 19th February. He planned to have the two professionals together, including a rapper and the other as a comedian/actor/writer. He finished releasing his first album, Professional Rapper, on 31st July 2015, featuring various artists like Hannibal Buress, Brendon Urie (Panic! at the Disco), and RetroJace, Snoop Dogg, Fetty Wap, T-Pain, and Rich Homie Quan.

Dave Burd and Mystikal and Trinidad James also appeared in one of the favorite Funny or Die videos, “Watch Yo Self,” in 2016. On 19th April 2019, he released another famous track, a single called Earth, where he met and featured artists like Justin Bieber, Ed Sheeran, Ariana Grande, and Shawn Mendes. All the artists have given voices to the animated personalities in the music video.
