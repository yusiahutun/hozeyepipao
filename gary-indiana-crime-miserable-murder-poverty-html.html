A travel blogger has captured the stark realities of what it's really like to live in Gary, Indiana - a city that has the country's lowest life expectancy and one of the highest homicide rates.

Peter Santenello, 45, a popular YouTuber who is based in the US, recently took a trip to the infamous area - which flourished in the '50s after it became home to the biggest steel mill in the world, but slowly transformed into a hotbed of crime and poverty, even being branded as the 'murder capital of the US' during the '90s.

He laid bare the area's quick rise to a booming town that produced iron ore for buildings all across America - as well as its harrowing descent to one of the most dangerous places to live after the industrial revolution stripped it of its importance.

He spoke to residents who described being terrified to walk down the streets at night out of fear of being robbed - or even shot - and showcased an array of decaying buildings and abandoned homes, raising the question of how the once-bustling city that was the home to over 200,000 people turned into a vessel of drugs, violence, and murder, which now has a population of a mere 69,000.

The city - located along the Southern shore of Lake Michigan, about 45 minutes from Chicago - first came into existence in 1906, when the Gary Works Steel Mill was opened, which was the 'biggest steel mill in the world' at the time.

The factory 'pumped in' money for the area and created tens of thousands of jobs - but everything changed in the '70s when the country started importing steel from overseas instead.

'In 1970, there were 30,000 workers at the plant, the biggest in the world at one time. But by 1990, there were only 6,000 workers, and now I believe there are even less,' Peter explained in a video shared to his YouTube account earlier this month.

'You'd think by its proximity to Chicago, being near the lake, that it probably has a strong future.

'But Gary is now the city with the lowest life expectancy in the United States. It also has one of the highest homicide rates.

'Gary's life expectancy is on average 71.4 years. The top in the country is 84.7 years, that being in Newtown, Massachusetts. 

'A lot of that has to do with economics, environment, gun violence. Gary's not doing so good in that department unfortunately.'

Back in 1993, the Chicago Tribune reported that Gary had become the 'murder capital of the nation.'

It had a record 110 killings that year, which gave it a murder rate of 91 per 100,000 residents - the highest in the country at the time. 

As of 2019, the murder rate had dropped to 76.3 per 100,000 people, which is still extremely high compared to the national murder rate of five per 100,000 people.

The city is famous for being the birthplace of the late singer Michael Jackson and his siblings - something the residents are extremely proud of.

'Gary has got a lot of different feelings. It's got the feeling of decay but also old world charm,' Peter reflected after his visit. 

'There's a depth and energy here. It feels like it's in a time standstill. I don't think there's anything like this, this is a very unique town.

'Gary, you can't really put a label on it. It has a lot of old world feel, time seemed to slow down there. It felt like it was removed from everything else.'

He also noted that everyone he interviewed was 'open-armed and easy to talk to,' despite its terrible 'reputation.' 

'It has a bad reputation but look at my day today, just going up and talking to people, everyone's been cool,' he concluded.

'There's definitely edge here, I'm not gonna deny that, there's some roughness to the place, but even in one of the top three cities of homicide, you can still come in here and talk to the people and they open up. 

'The vast majority of people here in Gary are great, hardworking people getting through life. 

'There are definitely some bad apples, I got some looks that said, like, don't even try to bring that camera around me.

'The main story of Gary is a town that's lost a lot of its population and a lot of its jobs. Is it at rock bottom or has it already rebounded a little? Does it have more down to go? I don't know.'
